The film centers around Sakthivel (played by Kamal Haasan), a powerful mafia kingpin, and his brother Manikkam. Their lives take an unexpected turn when they rescue a young boy named Amaran (Silambarasan) during a chaotic police shootout. They raise Amaran as their own son, shaping him into a loyal and capable member of their criminal empire. Years later, a ruthless assassination attempt forces Sakthivel to confront the consequences of his actions.

Consumed by grief and driven by a burning desire for retribution, Sakthivel embarks on a destructive path, systematically dismantling the very family he once cherished. The narrative cleverly explores the idea that sometimes, the greatest enemies aren’t external – they can be those closest to you. This father-son conflict is at the heart of “Thug Life,” and it's brilliantly portrayed by Haasan and Silambarasan.
